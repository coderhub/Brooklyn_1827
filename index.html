Apprentices Library

The Apprentices Library was an important building in Brooklyn because it housed the Village Trustees office, Kings county court, Municipal court, and Brooklyn savings bank.

Founded in 1823 by Augustus Graham

Image Reference:"Constitution of the Apprentices' Library Association of the village of Brooklyn.". Printed material. Brooklyn Museum.

Rope-Walk by Washington and Tillary

Joshua and Comfort Sands took advantage of this increase of maritime activity, starting a business building cordage and rigging equipment. The Sands brothers started this businesses by constructing rope walks in various areas on their land.
In the image below a European rope walk in the 1770's is depicted and covers a large area. This image may represent what tools the sands brothers were using on their ropewalks.
